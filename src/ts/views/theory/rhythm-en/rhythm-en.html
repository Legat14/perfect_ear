Rhythms

Music is made up of three elements: melody, harmony and rhythm. We have already learned quite a lot about melody and harmony (scales, chords). Now let's get to the rhythm.

Rhythm is a sequence of uniform or uneven pulsations.

Rhythm gives order to music, movement. Rhythm is something you can dance to or nod your head to.

We will begin our study of rhythm with some basic concepts.

  • Share - imagine a clock ticking every second, i.e. ticking 60 times per minute. Each tick is a share. When time is divided into equal intervals - each of them share. Thus, the beat is the basic unit of time. The beats are strong and weak. Strong beat - a beat on which the emphasis is placed in music to give rhythmic pulsation.
  • Meter is a sequence of strong and weak beats in a piece. A meter can be thought of as a ruler dividing time into equal intervals, which consist of even smaller intervals.
  • Tempo - the speed of alternation of beats. Tempo often refers to the speed at which a piece is played.
  • Note Duration - indicates the duration of the note.
  • Pause - silence in the melody. Pauses, like notes, have a duration. This means that every time you see a pause sign, you must stop for the duration of that pause.

Note Values

Depending on the duration, each note has a name. We will present the 5 most common durations.

Whole note

Whole - the longest note of all. In 4/4 time, it occupies all the beats.

Half

This duration, as you might guess, is equal to half a whole note. In 4/4 time, this note takes 2 beats.

Quarter note

A quarter is 4 times shorter than a whole note and 2 times shorter than a half note. In 4/4 time, such a note sounds exactly one beat.

Eighth

Eighth - half a quarter, occupies one half of the beat in 4/4. Two or more eighth notes can be combined with edges

Sixteenth

The sixteenth note is 16 times shorter than the whole note and is a quarter of a beat in 4/4.

Time Signature

Time signature is a value used in music to indicate the number of beats per bar and the value of each beat. The size is recorded at the beginning of the work.

Here the size is 4/4. There are many other sizes: 3/4, 6/8, etc. However, 4/4 is perhaps the most common.

The number at the top indicates the number of beats per measure. In our example, there are 4. If the time signature were 6/8, there would be 6 beats in each measure.

The number at the bottom indicates the length of each beat. If, for example, it is equal to 4, this means that each beat is equal in duration to a quarter note. If it is equal to, for example, 8, the share is equal to the eighth note.
