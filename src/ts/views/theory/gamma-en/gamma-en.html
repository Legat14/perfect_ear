Scales

After a detailed study of the concept of "Mode", you can safely proceed to the study of the concept of "tonality". After reading this article, you should have no problem playing absolutely any scale.

Recall that the progressively ascending or descending melodic movement of the scale of the fret within at least an octave is called gamma .

As we found out, a mode is a scale consisting of stable (I, III, V) and unstable steps (II, IV, VI, VII).

In professional European music of the 17th-19th centuries, and, partly, of the 20th century, two modes are most often used - natural major and natural minor. The first degree of the major and minor scale is called tonic , it is the center to which all other degrees and chords of the scale gravitate.

For example , if we build the F major and minor scales from the note F (based on the tonal structure of these modes), we will get the key of F major and F minor.

Note : the structure of the major scale: tone - tone - semitone - tone - tone - tone - semitone. The structure of the minor scale: tone - semitone - tone - tone - semitone - tone - tone.

Thus, tonality is a certain height position of a major or minor scale in a musical system. Each fret has twelve pitch positions, that is, twelve keys (12 major and 12 minor). Since the distance between the keys is not the same, accidental signs are necessary for the steps of keys ( signs of increase - sharps , and signs of decrease - flats ). Each tonality has its own permanent signs of alteration. As a rule, they are set at the key (treble, bass, tenor, etc.) and are called key signs.

For example , if we build from a note to a minor scale, then we will need to apply three flats - B-flat, E-flat and A-flat (Fig. 1). And if we decide to build an A major scale from the sound, then we will need to use three sharps: F-sharp, C-sharp and G-sharp (Fig. 2).

On the white keys, you can build only one major key - C major, and only one minor key - A minor. There are no key signs in these keys. Between themselves, these keys will be called parallel , since their step composition and the number of key characters are the same. In major, the parallel tonality is on the VI degree, in minor - on the III.

There is another definition of the major and minor modes, which is based on the simultaneous sounding of three stable steps (I-III-V) that form a chord - a triad. In the major key, a large triad is formed (B 5 3 ), in the minor key - a small one (M 5 3 ). Read more about these chords in the Chords: Triads section.

If we start building pure fifths upwards from the sound to and from the sound A (part 5), and from these sounds major and, accordingly, minor scales, we will get seven major and seven minor sharp keys. In each subsequent key, one key sign (sharp) will be added, starting with F-sharp.

Note : The secret to quickly memorizing signs in scales: in a sharp major key, each new sign is on the seventh step. Knowing the order of sharps, it will not be difficult to calculate the number. In A major, the 7th step is G, which means there are three sharps in this key: F-do-G. In the key of C-sharp major, the 7th step is si, which means that there are seven sharps in this key: f-do-sol-re-la-mi-si. Each new flat in major keys appears on the fourth step. So, for example, in the key of A-flat major, the fourth step is re, which means that there are four flats in this scale: si-mi-la-re. Keeping in mind the parallel keys, you can easily determine the number of characters in minor scales.

Three types of major and minor scales

Major and minor modes have varieties : natural mode, harmonic mode and melodic mode . Natural major and minor we considered in the section "Key".

In the harmonic major , the VI step is lowered by half a tone (see Fig. 5).

Note : If you need to lower the flat, then you must use the double-flat sign (double-reduction, indicated as ♭♭ ), if you need to lower the sharp, then you must use the becar accidental sign ( cancellation sign, indicated as). To increase the sharp, the double-sharp sign is used (twice-raise, denoted as ), to increase the flat, you must use the bekar.

Unlike natural major and minor, which have constant key marks, marks that occur in harmonic and melodic form will be called random alteration marks.

The use of these types of major and minor modes is a consequence of the development of the modal system in order to diversify it.
